# Dental Tourism Qualifier - Conversation Guide

## Overview

This guide provides conversation flows for the voice qualifier agent. The agent is designed to have natural, human-like conversations while qualifying leads for dental tourism sales. Use these frameworks as reference, not as rigid scripts.

---

## Opening Script (First 30 seconds)

**Initial Greeting:**
```
"Hi [Name]! I'm calling on behalf of Cancun Dental Partners. Do you have about 5 minutes? I'm reaching out because our research shows you might be interested in dental care options, and I wanted to see if there's a fit for you."
```

**Alternative (if colder list):**
```
"Hey [Name], thanks for picking up! This is [Agent Name] from Cancun Dental Partners. Quick question—are you currently exploring any dental work or procedures?"
```

**If they say no or are cold:**
```
"No worries at all! Would you be open to just a quick 3-minute conversation? I promise it'll be useful."
```

---

## Qualification Questions Framework

### Phase 1: Problem Discovery (2-3 minutes)

**Question 1: Identify Needs**
```
"Tell me, are you currently dealing with any dental issues, or is there dental work you've been considering?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Specific procedures (implants, crowns, veneers, etc.)
- Whether they're in pain or aesthetic-driven
- How long they've been thinking about it
- Previous treatment attempts

**Follow-up if vague:**
```
"Is this something that's been on your mind for a while, or is it more recent?"
```

---

**Question 2: Seriousness & Timeline**
```
"Have you had any consultations with a dentist about this, or are you still in the exploring phase?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Treatment plan already exists = warmer
- Just thinking about it = colder
- Urgency level

**Probe deeper:**
```
"If you decided to move forward, what would be your ideal timeline? Days, weeks, months?"
```

---

**Question 3: Budget Awareness**
```
"This might seem forward, but have you looked into what this would cost in your area?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Sticker shock = good positioning opportunity
- Budget mentioned = critical qualifier
- Willingness to invest

**If they mention a number:**
```
"Okay, got it. And if you could do the exact same work here in Cancun for 60-70% less, would that change the conversation?"
```

---

### Phase 2: Objection Anticipation (1-2 minutes)

**Question 4: The Travel Conversation**
```
"One thing I want to address upfront—how do you feel about the idea of traveling to Mexico for the procedure? Is that something that appeals to you, or is it a concern?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Travel appetite (vacation vs. burden)
- Real concerns vs. polite deflection
- Whether they have travel experience

**If negative:**
```
"I totally get it. Travel isn't for everyone. What specifically is the bigger concern—is it the logistics, time off work, or something else?"
```

---

**Question 5: Safety & Quality (The Trust Question)**
```
"The other thing people ask about is whether the quality is really the same. What's your biggest question about that?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Education level needed
- Trust in Mexico healthcare
- Need for credentials/proof

**If very skeptical:**
```
"Fair question. Our partner clinics are JCI-accredited—that's the same certification as major US hospitals. And our dentists trained in the US with 10+ years of experience. Would seeing patient testimonials or credentials help?"
```

---

**Question 6: The Readiness Question (Qualification Moment)**
```
"So if everything we've talked about made sense—the cost savings, the quality, the travel—what would make you comfortable taking the next step?"
```

**Listen for:**
- What's blocking them (price, timing, trust, spouse)
- Whether they're ready now or future
- Who else needs to be in the conversation

---

### Phase 3: Close Setup (30-60 seconds)

**Question 7: Permission to Proceed**
```
"Based on what you've shared, I think there's definitely something here for you. Would you be open to talking with our patient coordinator who can answer any final questions and show you the options?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Yes = HOT LEAD (transfer immediately)
- "Let me think about it" = WARM LEAD (get contact info)
- "No, not interested" = Cold or Not-Qualified

## Objection Handler Scripts

### "I'm concerned about quality / safety"

**Script A (Education-focused):**
```
"That's the most important question, actually. Here's what's real: our clinics are JCI-accredited, which means they meet the exact same standards as US hospitals. Your dentist will have been trained in the US and have 10-15 years of experience. Everything is documented, warranted, and if anything ever goes wrong, you get free repairs for 5 years."
```

**Script B (Social proof):**
```
"I get it. Honestly, that was the biggest concern for patients before they actually went. What I always tell people is: check the reviews independently. We have over [X] verified reviews online. Read them yourself. And if you want, we can connect you with patients from your state who've been through it."
```

**Script C (Comparison):**
```
"Here's the thing—the dentist you'd see in Cancun went to the same schools as dentists in the US. The equipment is the same. The standards are the same. The only difference is you're not paying the US overhead costs. So the care is equal or better, for a fraction of the price."
```

---

### "I'm worried about travel / logistics"

**Script A (Simplification):**
```
"I know travel feels like a hassle, but we actually handle all of it. We book your flight, arrange your hotel—which you can make as nice as you want, they're often beachfront—we pick you up from the airport, take you to the clinic, and back. You literally just show up. And most people combine it with a vacation, so it doesn't feel like 'medical tourism,' it feels like a trip where you happen to get your teeth fixed."
```

**Script B (Time comfort):**
```
"The actual procedure time is usually 2-3 visits over 7-10 days, depending on what you need. Between appointments, you're free to relax at your hotel, go to the beach, explore the city. A lot of people bring their family and make it a vacation. It's not like spending a week at the dentist's office."
```

**Script C (Social proof):**
```
"Thousands of Americans do this every year. We have patients from [your state] who've made the trip. They say the logistics are way simpler than they expected. Want me to connect you with someone who's already done it? They can tell you exactly how it works."
```

---

### "I'm not sure about the cost / my budget"

**Script A (Savings positioning):**
```
"I hear you. Let me put it differently: a lot of people think 'international = cheaper AND worse.' That's not the case. It's the same quality, just at a fraction of the cost. For example, a dental implant that costs $4,500 in the US costs about $1,200 here. Full implant, crown, everything. So you're getting the exact same thing for about a quarter of the price."
```

**Script B (Financing option):**
```
"If the upfront cost is the blocker, we have financing options. You could put down 30-50% before your trip and finance the rest over 6-12 months at reasonable rates. A lot of people use their HSA or FSA to cover it, which is pre-tax dollars. Does that make it more workable?"
```

**Script C (Pain-based):**
```
"I get it—it's a significant investment. But here's what I always ask people: what's it costing you to NOT do it? If you're in pain, or self-conscious about your smile, or dreading the dentist—that has a real cost to your quality of life. Sometimes the money question is really a confidence question."
```

---

### "I need to talk to my spouse / partner / family"

**Script (Enablement):**
```
"Totally makes sense. I don't want you to make this decision alone anyway. What I'd suggest is: let me send you some information, and maybe we schedule a time when you and [spouse] can both talk with our patient coordinator. They can answer any questions from both of you and walk you through the whole thing together. Sound good?"
```

---

### "I don't trust international healthcare"

**Script (Reframing):**
```
"I understand—that's a fair hesitation. Here's what might change your mind: our clinics are regulated by Mexican health authorities and certified by international bodies like JCI. That's actually MORE oversight than a random US dentist in a strip mall. Plus, all communication is in English, and our doctors are available for follow-ups by video. You're not going rogue—you're going to an accredited, professional facility."
```

---

### "I've heard horror stories about dental tourism"

**Script (Nuance):**
```
"Okay, so that's real—there ARE bad actors in the industry. Some fly-by-night operations where you can't trust the follow-up care. That's exactly why we're different. We've been doing this for [X] years, we're partner clinics, every procedure comes with a warranty, and you get lifelong access to your dentist via video consultation. We're not a Groupon deal—we're a partnership."
```

## Hot Lead Transfer Script

**When to transfer:**
- Explicit interest in proceeding to next step
- Budget confirmed ($3k+) OR financing accepted
- Timeline is 3 months or sooner
- No major unresolved concerns
- Enthusiasm is high

**Transfer Script:**

```
"Based on everything you've shared, I think we're a really strong fit for you. What I want to do is connect you with our patient coordinator, [Name]. They're going to walk you through the next steps, answer any remaining questions you have, and get your consultation scheduled. They'll also send you our detailed pricing, clinic photos, and patient testimonials. You good with me transferring you over right now? It should only take 10-15 minutes, and you can ask them anything."

[If yes]

"Perfect. Let me get you transferred. [Name] will be on in just a second. Thanks so much for your time, and I'm excited to get you on your way to getting your smile back!"

[Transfer]
```

## Warm Lead Closing Script

**When to use:**
- Genuine interest but some hesitation remains
- Budget or timeline unclear
- Need to do more research or talk to someone else
- Want to follow up later

**Script:**

```
"I think there's real potential here for you. I don't want to push you into anything—this is a big decision and you should take your time. Here's what I suggest: let me get your contact info, and our team will send you a detailed packet with pricing, clinic information, patient reviews, and our aftercare guarantee. Then we can schedule a callback with our patient coordinator in a few days or whenever you're ready. Sound fair?"

[Collect info]

"Great. We'll get this to you by [tomorrow/end of day], and someone will call you on [specific day] at [time] to see where you're at. If you have questions before then, just text us or call back. Deal?"
```

## Cold Lead Nurture Script

**When to use:**
- Interest exists but timeline is 6+ months out
- Budget not confirmed
- Curiosity-driven, not urgent
- Objections are moderate

**Script:**

```
"I appreciate you taking the time to chat with me today. It sounds like this is something on your radar, but the timing might not be perfect right now, and that's okay. What I'd love to do is stay in touch. I'm going to add you to our monthly updates—we send out information about new procedures, patient success stories, and financing options. If anything changes with your timeline or you get closer to making a decision, you just reach out. We'll be here. Sound okay?"

[Confirm email/phone]

"Perfect. You'll hear from us next month, but feel free to reach out anytime. Thanks again!"
```

---

## Not-Qualified / No-Interest Script

**When to use:**
- Clear "no" to dental work
- Extreme skepticism without openness
- Hostile or dismissive tone
- Not a good fit for product

**Script:**

```
"I hear you, and I totally respect that. Dental tourism definitely isn't for everyone. If things change down the road—maybe you have a different dentist recommend it, or circumstances change—feel free to reach out anytime. We'll be around. Thanks for taking the call!"
```

## Call Outcome Documentation

After each call, log these items in your follow-up system:

### HOT LEAD
- [ ] Name, phone, email confirmed
- [ ] Procedures listed
- [ ] Budget confirmed ($3k+)
- [ ] Timeline (next 3 months)
- [ ] Transfer time logged
- [ ] Coordinator notes added
- [ ] Follow-up deadline (within 24 hours)

### WARM LEAD
- [ ] Name, phone, email confirmed
- [ ] Procedures of interest noted
- [ ] Budget range captured
- [ ] Timeline (3-6 months)
- [ ] Main objection documented
- [ ] Follow-up date scheduled (3-7 days)
- [ ] Email packet sent
- [ ] Next call reminder set

### COLD LEAD
- [ ] Name, phone, email confirmed
- [ ] Interest level noted (research, future, price-checking)
- [ ] Timeline (6+ months)
- [ ] Main objection captured
- [ ] Nurture sequence started
- [ ] Next contact date (30 days)
- [ ] Quarterly email list added

### NOT-QUALIFIED
- [ ] Reason documented (not interested, already committed elsewhere, health contraindications, etc.)
- [ ] Exit was respectful
- [ ] Do-not-call flag set (if appropriate)

## Conversation Dos and Don'ts

### DO:
- Ask open-ended questions
- Listen more than you talk
- Use their name occasionally (but not excessively)
- Acknowledge concerns before solving them
- Share specific numbers and examples
- Offer choices rather than ultimatums
- Follow up exactly when promised
- Take detailed notes on their situation

### DON'T:
- Interrupt or talk over them
- Push too hard on timeline
- Make health guarantees
- Compare too aggressively with competitors
- Be overly familiar or casual (unless rapport allows it)
- Rush the call
- Fake understanding of medical terms
- Pressure them to decide right now

## Key Phrases That Work

**Building rapport:**
- "That's a really good question."
- "I hear you on that."
- "That makes total sense."
- "You're thinking about this the right way."

**Creating urgency (gently):**
- "While we have you, let me ask..."
- "This is actually the perfect time because..."
- "I'm glad you called now because..."

**Establishing credibility:**
- "Our patients consistently report..."
- "What we've found over [X] years..."
- "The data shows that patients who..."

**Closing:**
- "What would make this a 'yes' for you?"
- "If we solved that concern, would you be ready to move forward?"
- "Does that address your question?"

## Quality Assurance Checklist

For supervisors reviewing calls, ensure the agent:
- [ ] Built rapport in opening 30 seconds
- [ ] Asked at least 3 open-ended questions
- [ ] Identified specific procedures of interest
- [ ] Explored timeline and budget
- [ ] Addressed at least one objection with empathy
- [ ] Used specific pricing or savings examples
- [ ] Correctly qualified the lead (Hot/Warm/Cold)
- [ ] Appropriate next-step action taken
- [ ] Maintained professional but warm tone throughout
- [ ] Did not oversell or make unrealistic promises
- [ ] Total call time 5-12 minutes (efficiency + rapport)
